Three years ago, I didn't think racism really existed anymore

Yes yes, I know what you're thinking.

... or Sexism

That the World was a Pure Meritocracy

Those who didn't do well were just lazy or incompetent

My behavior towards girls may have made them feel excluded I casually said hurtful racist things, as if they were funny I had graduated from college and moving to the US, 22 at the time, old enough to know better IMO.

But Something Changed

I went from ignorance to awareness to talking about it to speaking here today!

... all this in the last three years

I want to talk about this change today

And the lessons learned

How experiences in the US led to the change, and how there were parallel situations in India where I was on the other side of the fence.

Growing up in India

The kinds of things I internalized growing up

India has a big marked for fairness creams, because apparently dark skin is bad.
Ads show you get jobs if you're fair skinned, regardless of competence.
Now for men too!
Ugh.

I found these things OK

I made jokes about girls

I said bad things about people who didn't look like me

I thought I was "better" than others

This is how I feel about it now.

Then I came to America

... and I was suddenly on the other side of the fence

On this Side of the Fence I ...

Didn't have a large group of friends around

Got exposed to the struggle to "belong"

Faced small and large instances of racism

Experienced failure due to personal struggle

Realized I could be smart and still fail

Accepted that circumstances can override efforts

Finally understood why talking about equality is important!

Comparing my experiences on the two sides of the fence. Microaggressions.
It was like snapping out of the matrix! I could never see the world the same way again. I could see that Indian and American societies were basically similar, and I had been blind to a lot of things all along.
